6/30: Kyoto’s Shrines and Temples　京都の神社と寺

Thousand Red Gates 伏見稲荷大社 (7:30AM)

The shrine sits at the base of a mountain, also named Inari, which is 233 meters (764 ft) above sea level, and includes trails up the mountain to many smaller shrines which span 4 kilometers (2.5 mi) and take approximately 2 hours to walk up all the way.

This shrine is always crowded by tourists, so it is best to be there early (at 7 to 8 AM). There is no need to go to the end, but if you would like there should be a view of Kyoto on the peak.

Yasaka Pagoda 八坂の塔 (9:00AM)

The Yasaka Pagoda, also known as the Tower of Yasaka, is a Buddhist pagoda located in Higashiyama-ku, Kyoto, Japan. The 5-story tall pagoda is the last remaining structure of a 6th-century temple complex known as Hōkan Temple. The pagoda is now a tourist attraction.

This alleyway shown in the photo is also usually crowded by tourists, so if you want any clear photo opportunities it is best to go early. If clear photos aren’t that important, it may be more convenient to go at the same time we are planning to head to Kiyomizu temple, because they are essentially a few minutes away from one other.

Note that this is located in the Gion District, which the Kyoto Government has restricted access for tourists in certain alleyways to prevent geisha harassment.

Kiyomizu Temple 清水寺 (11:00AM)

Kiyomizu-dera ("Pure Water Monastery") is a Buddhist temple located in eastern Kyoto, Japan. The temple is part of the Historic Monuments of the Ancient Kyoto UNESCO World Heritage Site.

The popular expression "to jump off the stage at Kiyomizu" is the Japanese translation of the English expression "to take the plunge". This refers to an Edo-period tradition that held that if one were to survive a 13-meter (43-foot) jump from the stage, one's wish would be granted.

There is a good view of the city and visitors can take a sip of mountain spring water from Otowa Waterfall 音羽の滝 using the long scoops provided at the site. Hold out the long scoops, which has been pre-sterilized in UV blue light, to catch one of the 3 streams of spring water. Each stream is said to bestow longevity, success, and love. Go early, as the queues for Otowa Waterfall can get windingly long.

See the website for more things to do at Kiyomizu Temple. https://plingthinks.com/2016/09/25/kiyomizu-dera-kyoto-temple-8-things-to-do/

Heian Shrine 平安神宮 (3:00 PM)

The Heian-jingu Shrine (平安神宮, Heian-jingū) is a Shinto shrine located in Sakyō-ku, Kyoto, Japan. The Shrine is ranked as a Beppyō Jinja (別表神社), meaning it is one of the most important shrines in all of Japan.

The history is rooted in the fact that it was built to commemorate the 1100th anniversary of Heian-kyo (平安京, Japan’s Old Capital) and honor Emperor Tenmu, the emperor that ruled over Kyoto at that time.

The Japanese-style garden in the land of Heian Shrine takes up about half the land area (approximately 33060 meters-squared). It consists of beautiful sakura and maple trees, along with many ponds and flowers.

Due to the commonality of crowding in Kyoto, especially in the Gion District where most of the attractions are located, it is much better to wake up early 6:30 AM or 7 AM to get to the points of interest first to enjoy them.

7/1: Day Trip to Nara　奈良の一日旅

Arriving in Nara By Train 奈良に行く (10:30 AM)

The train ride from Kyoto to Nara—specifically Kintetsu-Nara Station (近鉄奈良駅) takes around 45 minutes one way, and there is usually one train every 20 minutes. Assuming we arrive at the station at around 9 AM, we can safely get out of Nara Station at 10:30 AM.

Tonkatsu Ganko Nara とんかつがんこ奈良店 (11 AM)

This is a famous Tonkatsu restaurant that has a very high rating, opens at 11 AM on Mondays. This restaurant is very popular, so it is a good idea to eat it earlier (i.e. close to when we arrive at the station) before exploring the rest of Nara.

Nakatanidou Mochi Shop Show 中谷堂 (12 PM)

This shop is famous for its theatrical and traditional way of making mochi and has went viral over its captivating process of rhythmic shouting and hammering mochi on a wooden mortar. The line for the mochi is usually quite long, but it moves quite fast. However, the view for the mochi making may be more crowded and hard to see during peak times.

If you would like to see the viral video on YouTube, it is in the link here: Pounding Mochi With the Fastest Mochi Maker in Japan (24 million views on Great Big Story)

From all the reviews however, the mochi seems very delicious and worth the wait.

Kofuku-ji 興福寺 (1 PM)

Kofukuji (興福寺) used to be the family temple of the Fujiwara, the most powerful aristocratic clan during much of the Nara and Heian Periods. At 50 meters, the five-storied pagoda is Japan's second tallest wooden pagoda, just seven meters shorter than the five-storied pagoda at Kyoto's Toji Temple.

The complex is quite large, emcompassing a park, many buildings, and the famous Sarusawa Pond (猿沢池). This area is part of a large green space that connects the to the Asajigahara Enchi and then Nara Park, so we can take our time walking through it and enjoying the scenery.

Asajigahara Enchi 浅茅ヶ原園地 (2:30 PM)

Asajigahara-enchi Park, part of the extensive Nara Park, is known for its diverse landscape and cultural heritage. Located on the southern side of the park, near the first Torii gate of Kasuga Taisha Shrine, this hilly area offers stunning views, especially around the Sagiike Pond where the Ukimido Pavilion stands.

This is also quite close to Nara Park proper, so there are many deers in this area as well.

Kuki Cake 空気ケーキ (4 PM)

Kuki Cake is in a area close to Asajigahara Enchi and is a nice cafe to take a rest to eat some delicious pastries. VIsit the website below or look online for the menu!

http://www.kuukicake.com/

Nara Park 奈良公園 (5:30 PM)

Landscaped, public park with ancient temples & the Nara National Museum of art, plus tame deer. This is Nara Park proper, but essentially just another green space connected with the other places explored earlier in the day. You can feed the deer at Nara Park (along with Asajigahara Enchi) with special deer food that you can buy in the area. The deer are very polite and will bow for you to feed them, but be careful as sometimes they will get aggressive when you don’t give them what they want.

Kasuga Taisha 春日大社 (6:30 PM)

Kasuga Taisha is one of the most sacred sites in all of Japan. it attracts both devotees and tourists seeking an iconic Shinto experience. It was built during 768 AD, and is beautifully lit up at night. There is no entrance fee but it is a short walk (or hike) from Nara Park and the rest of the previously visited green space.

Wagyu Suji Curry 和牛スジカレー (8 PM)

This restaurant specializes in Japanese curry and omurice, and it receives very high ratings from both Japanese and foreigners. Check out their menu here: https://steak-cielbleu.com/

Bullet Train 新幹線

There are online tickets for the bullet train from Kyoto Station 京都駅 to Tokyo Station 東京駅, and the trip almost always consistently takes just less than 2 hours and 15 minutes. Assuming we take the train at 8:30 AM, we will need to wake up and check out around 7 to 7:30 AM and head to the station. We will arrive at Tokyo station around 10:45 AM.

If you want to buy a ticket where you can see Mt. Fuji outside the window, make sure to purchase the tickets for the LEFT SIDE of the train.

Shibuya 渋谷

SHIBUYA SKY 渋谷スカイ (2:00 PM)

Shibuya Sky is a 229 meter (751 feet) tall observation deck located on the 47th floor (top floor) of Shibuya Scramble Square Tower. The open-air deck offers a 360-degree panoramic view of Tokyo.

It is a place to take the most stunning photos, so make sure to bring you camera and phone. Unfortunately, tickets during the evening (which is the most popular) was all taken even 4 weeks in advance, but the view is still spectacular during the day.

The tickets I purchased are for TWO ADULTS and TWO HIGH SCHOOL STUDENTS, and the time to enter is 2:00-2:20. Therefore, we should get there around 1:45.

Shibuya Scramble Crossing 渋谷スクランブル交差点 (3:00 PM)

Shibuya Crossing, is a popular pedestrian scramble crossing in Tokyo. It is located in front of the Shibuya Station Hachikō exit and stops vehicles in all directions to allow pedestrians to inundate the entire intersection. Note that there is also a famous dog statue on one of the corners of the intersection.

This is right next to Shibuya Sky, so there will be almost no commute time between the two of them. There is also small cafes (also Starbucks) nearby where you can watch the crossing in action and get something to drink.

MEGA Don Quijote メガドンキホーテ (3:30 PM)

Main Store is a go-to shopping destination for international tourists in Tokyo, offering a vast range of affordable items, from daily essentials to souvenirs. It's one of Tokyo's largest stores and offers tax-free shopping for overseas visitors.

This expansive discount store offers an array of products, ranging from essential daily goods to groceries, home appliances, brand-name products, medicines, and souvenirs, all at incredibly reasonable prices!

Check out YouTube videos to see all the things to buy! A majority of the products are all very high quality! Note that if you want a tax refund, you are not allowed to open the packaging until you leave Japan.

CAFE: dotcom space Tokyo (5:00 PM)

This cafe is located near our next location (Meiji Shrine), and is in one of the most popular subdistricts—Harajuku 原宿. It is a few steps away from Takeshita Street 竹下通り, a popular point in Harajuku that features a variety of fashion and wacky, youth-driven shops selling colorful and crazy clothes and accessories.

The cafe itself features very aesthetic and delicious foods, and is a good place to rest before continuing on for the rest of the night. Note that this cafe closes at 6:30 PM, in case of any change to the day itinerary.

Meiji Shrine 明治神宮 (6:00 PM)

Meiji Shrine (明治神宮, Meiji Jingū) is a shrine dedicated to the deified spirits of Emperor Meiji and his consort, Empress Shoken. Located just beside the JR Yamanote Line's busy Harajuku Station, Meiji Shrine and the adjacent Yoyogi Park make up a large forested area within the densely built-up city and offer walking paths for a relaxing stroll away from the concrete.

Meiji Shrine closes at sunset, which is around 7:00 PM or later. It has no entrance fee so you are able to come and go as you wish. Due to its location next to the JR, it is very convenient to commute to Shinjuku, which is where the next set of locations are.

Shinjuku 新宿

Cross Shinjuku Vision 東新宿ビル クロス (7:30 PM)

Right outside the East Exit of Shinjuku Station, Cross Shinjuku Vision is an electronic billboard famous for its 3D effect. The most famous appearance on the billboard is the cat, which is pretty much an icon of Tokyo itself.

There is also a famous Cat Cafe in the building (hence the giant cat), and if you want to check that out the hours are from 1 to 9 PM. The area is surrounded by many fashion shops, restraunts, and other things that are sure to fill your time in the evening. However, doing so will cut into the time for the next area, which is similar in that it is mostly shopping. Feel free to adjust the time in each area to your liking.
